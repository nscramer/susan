You had to guess I was going to do a bit of makeup shopping whilst across the pond, right? Luckily, it was the more inexpensive products I was after. Being able to walk into a Kiko store was so exciting! I could have purchased so much more but honestly, I did get a bit overwhelmed. However, the process of building my Inglot palette might go down as when I was the most indecisive I've ever been (sorry, Nick). 

But, I'll cut to the chase. Here's what I got! 

From Kiko:
Water Eyeshadow in 200: This is quite possibly the most beautiful eyeshadow I own. I mean, would ya look at it?! It's a perfect mid-toned bronze shade that's not too gold and not too brown. Also, it's metallic power is strong. Dry, it adds a wonderful sparkle, and wet, the intensity is really kicked up. I highly recommend this product! 

Extra Sculpt Volume Mascara: I knew I had to grab this because I've heard Kathleen Lights rave about it so much. If I'm being honest, however, I'm not that impressed. It does give some great volume. The formula is definitely something I can get behind. For me, the wand is where the problems are. It's huge. So, so big. Also, the hourglass shape confuses me a bit because it means i'm putting one of the widest points of the wand into the inner corner—one of the smallest spaces of my eyes. I just found it a bit messy. I have to be light handed and move pretty slowly to avoid getting this all over my face. It could be user error, but it's not my favorite. 

From  Inglot: 
Walking up to the products, I knew I was going to have to make my own palette. I decided that 5 shadows would be a good place to start! I also knew that I wanted to grab one of their sculpting powders...which snowballed into the pretty little blush that also made it's way into my bag. Here's the shades:

Eyeshadows
#35 White, shimmery color for highlighting brow bone + inner corners. 
#357 Matte transition shade, the perfect mid-toned brown!
#154 Shimmery, metallic bronze. Hello, my go-to eyeshadow. 
#335 Matte burnt-orange & #299 Matte burgundy:  I stepped out of my box a bit with these two, and I'm so glad I did! I really love using these shades for some autumnal color in an eye look. I never thought I would like such warm shades on my complexion, but I stand corrected! 
I did notice I need to use an eye primer with these shadows. 

Sculpting powder: #505, a perfect shade for my pale Fall + Winter skin. 
Blush: #55 . This is a very cool toned pinky-mauve which is fitting for the cooler months! It gives a gorgeous flush of color to the face.
