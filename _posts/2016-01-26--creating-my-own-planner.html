In the beginning of December, the dreaded feeling of realizing your planner is coming to an end started to come over me. I was about to finish my fourth Moleskine Weekly Planner. A product which I do love very much and would recommend to anyone looking for a classy, simple way to organize their days. This time, though, I wanted to try something that offers a little more.
I've seen plenty of planners out there for different goals and focus areas. Most popular right now, I think, is the Erin Condren Life Planner. Now, I looked these up and down, and just wasn't sure I was into the myriad of colors going on. I prefer a minimal, clean look to planners.
There’s also the Get To Work Book, which is probably the closest thing to what I wanted, and as you can see was an inspiration for the design elements of mine. So, these two in mind, I set about my pro-con list to decide which one to purchase. I wanted to do some goal tracking on a monthly basis, and I wanted to include fun things as well.
When it came down to it, I couldn’t pull the trigger on either of them. I just didn’t feel 100% in on them. And, if you’re type A like me, you know what i’m talking about. A planner is something you’ll carry around every day for an entire year. Not to mention, I tend to hang on to them for a while after they’re full, like documentation of what I did the year before. I find them pretty cool to look back on occasionally.

Now, there’s nothing really wrong with any of the planners I mentioned above, it’s just that they were either lacking something I wanted, or had too many extras that I wouldn’t get use out of.
This lead me to try my hand at creating my own planning system. So, I started mocking up what I thought I wanted my days to look like in a planner. I took inspiration from many planners I’d surveyed in my search, and landed on a very simple set up of a schedule, to-do list, and a section for notes. I later ended up adding a section to take note of what was for dinner each day, as a goal I have for this year is to get more adventurous in the kitchen.

I printed out a few of these days to test them out in the first work week of the year, and I really liked them. I started thinking that I actually might just go for it and make my own—get it professionally printed and bound and everything. From there, a friend of mine sent me a link to this post, where the author talks about how he used a site called blurb.com to make his own planner and get it printed for super cheap. This was the golden ticket! That same friend also happens to be a designer (check out her Esty shop!), and she offered to help me with the design of my planner. We got it set up into this order:
Photo pages
Monthly Calendars + Plans
Weekly Overviews
Daily Pages
Monthly Reflections

We messed around with the look of it and how everything flowed, but I was pretty positive about what I wanted so it didn't take long. It cost about $26 using Blurb to get it color printed and hard bound. Score.
And can I say—I’m obsessed with how it turned out!
The paper quality is really top notch; there is absolutely zero bleed through from my trusty Pilot Precise V5's and really no ghosting either. 

I only ordered the beginning of the year through the end of June, so if I want any changes I can make those for the second half of the year. I will likely have an update style post coming in a few months to let you know how i'm liking it and what I would change! 

What planning system do you swear by?
