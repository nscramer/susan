Yesterday was my twenty-fifth birthday. Wowza! I used to think 25 was so old. I remember when my oldest cousin was this age, and I thought she was so cool and independent. (She still is, btw). I just never imagined this day would come back then! 

You always have visions while growing up about what being an “adult” would be like. I can tell you, I couldn’t have predicted this at all. 

I’m 25. I have a loving and fulfilling relationship, an amazing dog, a career I can work hard at, a family who’s there for me always, and amazing friends. These people all chose to gather with me and celebrate this milestone with me, and I was unable to stop from getting a bit sappy. There’s so much going on in the world today. It’s an ugly, terrifying place in some respects. But in others, it’s bright and full of potential. I don’t force myself to step back and look at it all often enough, I don’t think anyone does. 

At this point in my life there are challenges, but they’re nothing I can’t handle—nothing that i’m not excited to face head-on. I’ve travelled, read, danced, cried, loved, and laughed through 25 years of life, and for that I am grateful. 

I wanted to spend time today addressing my 30 before 25 list. As you may know, I was able to cross a good amount off of that list, but, I didn’t complete it—yet. Here’s the thing about this list. I set out with high hopes. If you check it out, you’ll see I created it when I graduated college. I was 21 then. Needless to say, goals change! I did amend the list slightly as time went on, but there were some things on there that I didn’t get to. Some I should have, but some I feel okay about missing. 

Will I create another list? Yes. Will it likely have some items that are migrated from the old list? Yes. 

Here’s a look at what I completed: 

Attend a music festival (Midpoint Music Festival, October 2014)
Move into my own apartment (March 2014)
Land a job I enjoy and take pride in (January 2014)
Travel to France (October 2014)
Write for the blog twice a week (with a one month benchmark to create the habit) (March + April 2016)
Contribute writing to another blog (The Ahalogy blog; multiple)
Create a manageable workout regimen that makes me feel great. (began training with Jill in Dec. 2014)
Grow my hair out to mid-back length (it's about there, i'd say! April 2015) (and I just cut it off! HA! July, 2016)
Get a dog ([Fitz came home in August 2015!)
Visit NYC (April 2014)
Clean out closet and donate what I don't keep (Cut clothing in half in Jan. 2014) 
Read 30 new books
Make spaghetti squash (April 2015—NO idea what took me so long!)
Get a nice camera and put more time into taking photos—dreaming of the Olympus Pen E-PL7 (JULY 2015, Olympus Pen E-PL7 Purchased!)
Visit San Fransisco (May 2014) 
Lead a major project start to finish (work) (Brand Voice and Tone Guide, Jan. 2014)
Visit Harry Potter World at Universal Studios (Went with my mom in Oct. 2015!)

And, what I didn’t: 

Buy a new (personal) apple computer
Purchase the top 10 items on my high-end beauty wishlist
Visit Seattle
Take Nick to Denver
See Dave Matthews Band at the Gorge
Create my dream home office space
Take my mom to Europe or Belize
Read every Hemingway novel (The Old Man and The Sea, The Sun Also Rises,)
Run a 10k
Create a "Before I Die..." wall in Cincinnati and maintain it for a month.

There are some things on there that were definitely attainable, but in a few instances I let life take me different directions. I feel great about what I did accomplish and how I was able to use this list to guide where I spent my time (and funds) through the last four years. 

I’m hoping to my next list out very soon. And I think i’ll be extending this one to 30. 30? Weird. 
I’m excited to reveal that once it’s all completed!

Cheers my friends, to 25 great years past and many more to come!
