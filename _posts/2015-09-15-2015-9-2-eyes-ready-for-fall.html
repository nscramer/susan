When it comes to makeup looks for specific seasons, I tend to think those rules can and should be broken. If you want to wear a bold, dark lip in the summer, or a really light coverage & glowy look in the winter—why not? You do you, my friend. 

Lately, however, i’ve been getting really into cooler, darker tones in both my clothing and makeup. Breaking back into my olive green sweaters and deep purple eyeshadows. There’s no doubt this is a product of the changing seasons. Fall has always been my favorite, and I am extremely sad that Cincinnati fall’s seem to be getting shorter and shorter. (we’re looking at you, climate change) 

While i’m totally into these deeper shades on my lids (and the lips, if we’re being honest) once again, I have been pairing them with some more “summer appropriate” colors to help it be more of a transitional look. 

To achieve this, i’m back to being obsessed with my MAC Burgundy Times Nine palette. If you haven’t seen the MAC "Times Nine" range you’re seriously missing out. (I believe they're exclusively sold from Nordstrom, along with a few other special MAC palettes) They have them in a few different color collections, and they’re only $40. Let me say that again: Forty. Dollars. Now, these pans are a little smaller than the full-size MAC shadows we’re all used to, but I still think it’s an amazing deal—there's nine shadows in there, people! 


When I purchased this I didn’t think I’d get any use out of the shimmery gold color in the top left called Gold Lust. As the summer came through, let me tell you, it’s my most used shadow in the palette. You can tell I’ve made a serious dent in it. It's a wonderful warm, truly golden hue that looks so beautifully beachy. It does have a pretty chunky glitter, and I definitely recommend a primer or eyeshadow base underneath. In the Summer,  I’d let it stand alone all over the lid and a little on the lower lash line, tons of mascara & done. Now, as it gets cooler, this is where the other shades in this palette come into play. Lately, i’ve been starting with that same summery look, but now adding some of Haux (bottom row; far left), medium-toned matte plum color, through the crease & Sketch (bottom row; far right), a deep plum, in the outer-v. I'll always use a clean, fluffy brush at the end to blend out any harsh lines. 

From there, I’m a creature of habit. Slapping on a couple (or 5) coats of mascara (currently addicted to the L’Oreal Telescopic Carbon Black) and that’s my almost fall eye look sorted.
