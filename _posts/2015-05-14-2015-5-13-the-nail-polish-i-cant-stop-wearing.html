When it comes to nail polish, i've been known in the past to be a bit of a hoarder. I would buy a new color every time I was in the store, I built up quite the collection. Brights, neutrals, darks—I had them all.

I recently did a nail polish clear out, where I gifted a ton of colors. I had to get very real with myself about how much I would actually wear something. In doing this, I found that I was only left with neutrals, dark berry shades, and reds. Since it's getting warmer, i'm definitely gravitating towards the neutrals. I have a lot of nudes, browns, and pastels. Out of these i've had one color on my nails for about 2 months straight. When it chips, I take it off and promptly reapply.

Alright, alright, i'll tell you what it is: Revlon Colorstay Gel Envy in the shade Checkmate.

It's a gorgeous pale nudey-beige. The perfect nude, I'd venture to say. I'm a pale girl, and it doesn't wash out or blend in with my skin (which is a plus, shout out to my fellow pale gals). 

The formula looks opaque with just two coats, and it lasts really well without chipping. I've been using it with a Seche-Vite top coat, and I'm pretty much addicted.
