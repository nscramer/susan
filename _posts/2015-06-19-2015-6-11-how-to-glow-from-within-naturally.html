Everyone knows that contouring and highlighting have been all the rage as of late. While I like a lil’ sculpt for a night out, i’m definitely not one to do this on a daily basis. I do, however, firmly stand behind a nice glowy complexion to make a gal look healthy. I have very oily skin. Let me repeat: very oily. I’ve also got some dehydrated patches, it’s very sensitive, and breakout-prone. Joy! While I tend to lean more towards fuller coverage matte finish foundations, there are a few lighter & dewier ones I enjoy slapping on from time to time as well. No matter the coverage and finish, I always use this one tactic when i’m looking for a lit from within—not disco ball—glow.

The (not-so) secret?: Applying both highlighter and blush underneath your foundation.

Won’t the foundation just cover it up, you ask? That depends on the products and colors you use.

For the full coverage & matte foundations:

Since this foundation’s job is to combat shine and discoloration, I go a little bit clowny, but only for a moment. For this, I like using the NYX Born To Glow liquid illuminator in the shade Sunbeam. This is a very cool toned, pale pink highlight with serious sheen and shimmer. There’s also a hint of glitter in it (which is why I don’t use it on top of a base). For blush, I go for the NXY Rouge Cream Blush in the shade Glow. It’s quite the bright pink, and does look scary at first, there’s no denying. I apply this either with a small stippling brush or just my fingertips, and blend it out. Here’s where you look like a clown for those few moments. After that’s all sorted, I go in with the foundation, usually the Tarte Amazonian Clay (seriously has magical properties), and when it’s all buffed & blended, i’m left with a very natural, healthy looking glow and flush of color on the cheeks.

For a more light-weight & dewy base:

I go for a more toned down highlight and blush, but still apply in the same way. I’ve been loving the new Bare Minerals Complexion Rescue as the base for this type of look, I like it cause it helps my skin to stay looking like skin, and sets beautifully under a powder. I’ll grab the Becca Shimmering Skin Perfector in the shade Moonstone as a highlight. While it’s still an absolutely gorgeous highlight, it’s a little warmer and more of a golden-y sheen that won’t blind anyone. Sometimes I want to slather this stuff all over (okay, so I have done it—and loved it). For the blush i’ll grab another NYX Rouge Cream Blush, but this time in the shade Natural. It’s much more muted, and has some beige tones to it, so it’s a tad bit warmer. These two products translate perfectly under a dewy base. Want to have the dewy glow without looking like you’ve just dumped water on your face? Try a very finely milled setting powder, like the NXY HD Finishing Powder. It combats extra shine but still lets all of that luminosity come through.
