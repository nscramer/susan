I had a bit of a stroll through the mac counter last week and while I told myself to stay strong, I caved a bit. Who’s surprised? Not me. To justify the spend a bit, I will say, I stuck to picking up some staples. I have tried mac products many times in the past, and always been pretty loyal to their brushes when I want to go high end. Who doesn’t own multiple 217’s?. Luckily, my local mac counter was only out of one of the items I had my eyes on, which is a rare occasion when you’re looking for their cult products! 

I ended up picking up two lipsticks in the shades Patisserie and Velvet Teddy, one lip pencil in Soar, and one eye kohl in Teddy. 

I’m not sure how much explanation any of these beauties require; but i’ll give it a quick stab anyhow. 

Patisserie (Lustre): This is a great everyday shade to have on hand for any makeup look. It’s a light peachy-pink with a bit of gold shimmer running through it. Since it’s a lustre finish it’s a bit more sheer and light. If you’re someone who likes to have a simple lip on rather than going bare—you’ll get a ton of use out of this one. 

Velvet Teddy (Matte): Swoon. On the matte side of the spectrum, this little number is my new obsession. It’s such a subtle muted beige color but it doesn’t wash me out like I was suspecting it might. The original matte formula from Mac is comfortable and long wearing. 5 stars from me!  

Soar: As everyone else has this guy, I figured there was something to it. While it is a beautiful formula and great color, I feel like I have some way cheaper dupes in my collection. You might see a post about this soon. It’s pretty much the same color as my lips, so it goes well under most nude or pink shades for me. Definitely one I will get use out of, but might not spend the cash on it a second time. 

Teddy: Swoon again. Another one I’ve been wearing since I brought this little loot home. If you have blue eyes—you need this eyeliner. A warm, dark, chocolatey brown with a bronze shimmer in it, it’s a dream for making lighter peepers really stand out. I like that it’s definitely a brown, but it’s still dark enough to create those classic smokey eye looks with. I’ve been lining my top eyelid with this and using a pencil brush to really blend it out. Then I either leave it at that or I’ll pop a champagne shade in the center of the eyelid to add to the dimension of the look. The wear time is wonderful and all around I’m very happy with this one.
