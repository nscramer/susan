Ole Henricksen Ultimate Lift Eye Gel Roll On—This was a great find at my local TJ Maxx! Whenever i’m there I scour the cosmetics and skincare aisles for gems like this. (don’t worry, this doesn’t mean it’s hard to get—you can find it on amazon!) I scored this for around $10, and I really like it! The roller ball is soon nice and cooling. I’d be lying if I said I’d love just the metal roller ball even if there wasn’t a nice eye gel in there as well! The gel itself is so light and refreshing. I only use this in the mornings, and I find it really helps my eye bags, helping me to look less like I haven’t slept in 5 years. If you can get ahold of this one—I highly recommend it! 

Milani Prime Shield (mattifying and pore-minimizing)—I picked up this primer thinking it could be similar to something like the Smashbox photo finish primer, which I’ve always enjoyed. I know some people really aren’t into the silicone-feel primers, but personally I love them! These formulas do a great job of filling in my larger pores and smoothing out my skin. I had high hopes for this Milani offering, as most of their products which I’ve tried have pleasantly surprised me. 
I’ve been trying this one out for a few weeks and I’m still kind of “meh” about it, really. I’ll likely use it up, as I find my foundation applies nicely overtop, and it does smooth out some of the texture in my skin. However, in comparison to similar formulas, I find it a bit oily in texture. Also, it’s meant to mattify oily skin and while it helped a bit around my nose, my forehead ended up much more oily by the end of the day than normal. 
I also can’t say that it helped my makeup last any longer, but I wasn’t necessarily expecting that, that’s what setting sprays are for!
All in all, I think it’s a good option for smoothing the skin, but not for oil control. As I said, i’ll probably use this up, or pass it on to a friend with slightly drier skin than mine.
