This past weekend was Nick + I's anniversary, and he surprised me with a weekend trip to Nashville! It's a city I've been to a few times when I was younger, but this time we explored the parts of the city which are far from the famous honky-tonks on Broadway. 

We stayed at the Aloft hotel; the place had a very cool vibe with live music in the bar. (not uncommon for this town) When walking around we saw a lot of both Vanderbilt and Belmont's campuses, which are of course beautiful! Near Vanderbilt in Hilsboro Village, we gawked over the line at Pancake Pantry, ended up getting an awesome breakfast at Fido instead, and had a quick coffee/tea break at Revelator (highly recommend this place!). 

There's another neighborhood not far from Hilsboro called 12 South that's full of beautiful shops, taprooms, and eateries. There's such a great vibe to this neighborhood, and the weather was perfect so everyone was out and about. 

An interesting thing about this area is that many of the shops and restaurants seem to be in old auto service stations, which gives a really open vibe with a feeling of history, like the place has been lived in and seen some things. We loved it. A few notable shops are Imogene + Willie, S'more Love Bakery, and Amelia's Flower Truck.
